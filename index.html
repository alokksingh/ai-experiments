Questions? We have answers.

What is Nathan's current role at Adobe? ▾

Nathan Etter is the Senior Vice President at Adobe, overseeing global strategy, growth, product, and engineering for Adobe.com. He is responsible for the primary digital channel through which millions of creatives and businesses discover, evaluate, and purchase Adobe products globally.

What companies did Nathan work at before Adobe? ▾

Nathan has held leadership roles at The Walt Disney Company (VP, Digital Commerce), Zynga (VP Product Management), eBay (Senior Director, Product Management), and earlier-career positions at Mercury, Kintana, and Accenture. His career spans consumer, enterprise, gaming, and media industries.

Where did Nathan study? ▾

Nathan holds a B.S. in Biological Sciences from Stanford University, where he concentrated in Neuroscience and Animal Behavior alongside extensive Computer Science coursework. He also completed executive education at The Wharton School and Stanford Graduate School of Business.

What's Nathan's connection to the Marine Mammal Center? ▾

Nathan joined the board of The Marine Mammal Center in 2021. His passion for marine life stems from his undergraduate research at Stanford's Hopkins Marine Station in Monterey Bay, where he spent months studying Octopus rubescens — which he affectionately calls his spirit animal.

What is Nathan's role in Adobe's generative AI strategy? ▾

Nathan is a key champion of Adobe's generative AI initiatives, including Adobe Firefly, the content supply chain across Experience Cloud and Creative Cloud, and Adobe GenStudio. At Adobe Summit 2024, he was part of the team that unveiled major AI innovations across Adobe's product portfolio.

How did Nathan end up in product management from biology? ▾

Though a biology major at Stanford, Nathan took as many CS courses as possible. After graduating, he spent a decade writing code but discovered he loved influencing product direction and interacting with customers. Product management offered the perfect blend of technology and customer empathy — and he hasn't looked back since.
